Space

a mysterious and little-known area in our world. Since ancient times, a person, looking up at the sparkling stars, dreamed of learning all the secrets of outer space, and most importantly, to visit there, to find out if there is life other than our planet. Gradually, the cosmos revealed one mystery after another, scientists learned a lot of new things, but by now, humanity knows only a small part of the mysteries of the cosmos.

Galaxies

This is a cluster stars, planets, gas, dark matter and others objects related to each other gravity. There are several types of galaxies Spiral, Elliptical, Lenticular, incorrect and othres. Based on galaxy lies black hole, which create arrays around themselves with their power.

Milky Way

This is the Galaxy in which we live, it is a representative of a Spiral Galaxy. It has a well-defined core and a flat disk in which spiral arms are located. These arms are clusters of stars, gas and dust, where new stars are actively forming.

M87

Elliptical galaxies have an elliptical (rounded or oval) shape. They lack noticeable spiral arms and there is little star formation. They consist mainly of old stars and contain little gas and dust. Elliptical galaxies can be of various shapes — from almost spherical to highly elongated.

Solar system

It is a planetary system that includes the Sun and all the celestial bodies orbiting it. The solar system consists of 8 planets, dwarf planets, satellite planets, asteroids, comets and cosmic dust. All objects are held together by the gravity of the Sun. Eight planets revolve around the Sun in elliptical orbits and are located at different distances from it, creating unique conditions on each of them

Mercury

  • The closest planet to the Sun
  • The smallest planet in the Solar System
  • Has a very thin atmosphere, causing extreme temperature variations from hot to very cold
  • The surface is covered in craters, similar to the Moon

Venus

  • The second planet from the Sun, similar in size to Earth
  • Has a dense atmosphere of carbon dioxide, causing an extreme greenhouse effect on the surface
  • It is the hottest planet with temperatures up to 470°C
  • The atmosphere is full of sulfuric acid, and clouds completely cover the surface

Earth

  • The third planet from the Sun and the only one known to harbor life
  • Has an oxygen-rich atmosphere and liquid water
  • Earth's surface is covered with oceans and land, and the planet has a moon — the Moon
  • We live here, in case you didn’t know

Mars

  • The fourth planet from the Sun, known as the "Red Planet" due to iron oxides on its surface
  • Has a thin atmosphere composed mainly of carbon dioxide
  • The surface is filled with volcanoes, canyons, and polar ice caps made of frozen carbon dioxide

Jupiter

  • The fifth and largest planet in the Solar System
  • A gas giant composed mostly of hydrogen and helium
  • Has the famous Great Red Spot — a massive storm that has lasted for centuries
  • Has more than 79 moons, including the largest — Ganymede

Saturn

  • The sixth planet, known for its magnificent rings made of ice and space debris
  • Also a gas giant, primarily composed of hydrogen and helium
  • Has more than 80 moons, the largest being Titan

Uranus

  • The seventh planet from the Sun, rotates "on its side," making its rotation axis unique
  • An ice giant composed of hydrogen, helium, and methane, giving it a bluish tint
  • Has rings and 27 known moons

Neptune

  • The eighth and farthest planet from the Sun
  • Also an ice giant with strong winds, the most powerful in the Solar System
  • Has a deep blue color due to methane in its atmosphere
  • Neptune has 14 moons, the largest being Triton

Black Holes

These are cosmic objects with such strong gravitational pull that not even light can escape them. They form when very massive stars reach the end of their life and collapse under their own gravity.

Features of Black Holes

  1. Event Horizon — the boundary around a black hole beyond which nothing, including light, can escape. This is the "point of no return." Anything crossing the event horizon is absorbed by the black hole.
  2. Singularity — at the center of a black hole is a point of infinitely high density and gravity — the singularity. All known laws of physics break down at this point.
  3. Accretion Disks — some black holes can have disks of gas and matter around them, called accretion disks. Matter in these disks heats up to extreme temperatures and emits X-rays and other radiation before falling into the black hole.
